Tom seeks open data work.

As you may have realized, I’ve recently been studying data about open data so that we can use data to drive our opening of data. It has been more fun than many other things I’ve done, and I want to keep doing this research all day. So now I’m looking for work that will allow that.

Things I can do for you

There are a lot of things about open data that I want to look at, and they can be aligned with quite a many different efforts/organizations.

If you work for a data-related software company, I can prototype or implement new features in your product to make open data better. This could involve things like checking for out-of-date data and new ways of searching for data.

If you work for an organization that wants to release open data or share data internally, I can quantify how well your efforts are going, figure out what’s working, and find ways of making things go better. I’ve previously looked at whether datasets are appropriately licensed, how in-browser charting tools are used, and what sorts of data are available in a data catalog.

If you are giving grants in relation to open data, we should talk. I have a long list of things that I want to do, and it’s way more than I can possibly do; I can prioritize them somewhat based on your interests.

Interested?

If anything above sounded interesting to you or if you think you have something interesting that I haven’t thought of, email me at _@thomaslevine.com.
